New to Estonian OÜs?

What's an OÜ, and why Estonia?

An OÜ (osaühing) is Estonia's private limited company — the country's most common business form, and a popular choice for freelancers, consultants, and small software businesses who run an EU company remotely via Estonia's e‑Residency program. Estonia's headline feature: a company pays no corporate tax on profit it keeps or reinvests — tax is only due when profit is distributed (for example, as dividends).

Salary vs dividends — the choice this tool models

As an owner who also works in your OÜ, you can take money out two ways, and most people blend them:

  • Salary — taxed like ordinary Estonian employment (the company pays social tax on top; income tax, pension and unemployment are withheld). It costs more in tax, but it builds your state pension and health insurance.
  • Dividends — your share of company profit, taxed at a flat 22/78 (~22% of the gross) at the company level. No social tax — but none of the salary-based benefits, and it can only come from actual profit.

The slider lets you try any split and see exactly what reaches your pocket.

Sources & references

All rates are for 2026, primarily from the Estonian Tax and Customs Board (EMTA). The superscript numbers in the breakdown link to these sources.

  1. Social tax 33%, and the 2026 minimum monthly obligation (€886 base × 33% = €292.38) — emta.ee — Social tax
  2. Unemployment insurance premiums: employer 0.8%, employee 1.6% (fixed 2025–2028) — emta.ee — Unemployment insurance premiums
  3. Mandatory funded pension (II pillar): your 2% contribution, plus the 4% the state adds from social tax (6% total on a salary) — emta.ee — Funded pension contributions
  4. Basic exemption €8,400/yr (€700/mo), flat from 2026 with no income phase-out — emta.ee — Calculation of basic exemption
  5. Personal income tax 22% (the planned rise to 24% was cancelled in December 2025) — emta.ee — Tax rates
  6. Dividend / distributed-profit tax 22/78 — 22% of the gross (the reduced 14/86 rate was abolished in 2025) — emta.ee — Taxation of dividends
  7. Standard VAT 24% (permanent since 1 July 2025; applies to invoices to EE clients) — emta.ee — Value added tax
Estimates based on Estonian tax rules effective 2026. The funded pension (II pillar) is withheld from your salary — it lowers your take-home cash here, but it's your own retirement savings, not a tax.
